Is AI Music Flattening Global Culture?

A Computational Study of AI-Generated Music on Spotify Across 74 Countries

About This Research

AI-generated music is growing rapidly on streaming platforms — in some countries, AI tracks account for up to 50% of local charts.

This project asks whether AI music is expanding access to diverse musical styles, or accelerating "cultural flattening" across countries. Using audio embeddings from the MuQ-Mulan model and data from Spotify Viral 50 charts across 74 countries, we compare the geographic distribution of AI-generated and human-made music to understand how AI reshapes the global music ecosystem.

Methods & Data

14,800 tracks (2,045 AI-generated + 12,755 human-made) collected from Spotify Viral 50 charts across 74 countries.

Tracks are represented as 512-dimensional audio embeddings, then analyzed using variance decomposition (η²) and permutation testing to measure how much of the variation in music style is explained by country. Text-music cosine similarity is used to identify which mood and style descriptors best characterize AI music versus human music.
